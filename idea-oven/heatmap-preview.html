Next 30 days

One execution arc across both lists. Pick a primary play and a cash-flow side play, prove demand, deliver one small win, then double down on the strongest signal.

Days 1–3

Pick + frame

  • Pick one primary play and one cash-flow side play.
  • Make proof, not a giant app. Sample, demo, or one-page site.
  • FinFree stays live or waitlist only — do not let it eat the week.
Days 4–7

Talk to humans

  • Outreach to 25–50 real people in the target niche.
  • Collect objections verbatim — do not argue with them.
  • Rewrite the offer based on what people actually say, not what you wished they said.
Week 2

First paid pilot

  • Try to get the first paid pilot or customer.
  • If no one bites, narrow the offer — tighter niche, sharper outcome, smaller scope.
  • Do not lower the price as the first move.
Week 3

Deliver + capture

  • Deliver one small win for that first customer, end-to-end.
  • Capture the testimonial, screenshots, or before/after proof.
  • Kill anything that needs too much capital or fake confidence to keep going.
Week 4

Double down

  • Double down on the play with the strongest signal: closes, deposits, repeat asks.
  • Decide what becomes the next live landing page (replaces or joins FinFree).
  • Park the rest. They can come back later as version-two pages.

The 13-day sprint pattern

Same shape every time: prove fast, sell aggressively, collect deposits, fulfill, turn delivery into proof.

Days 1–3

Prove

  • Pick one wedge, one buyer, one painfully clear outcome.
  • Build one spec asset or demo — not a deck, an artifact.
  • Hire the smallest contractor team that can ship a sample.
  • Land on a single price the buyer can say yes to in one call.
Days 4–7

Sell

  • Send 50–100 personalized outreaches per day across both founders.
  • Use spec work and tiny demos as bait, not free consulting forever.
  • Lead with a result, not a feature list. Audit videos beat decks.
  • Book discovery calls inside 48 hours of first contact.
Days 8–11

Collect

  • Demand 50–100% upfront on pilots. Cash gap is the enemy.
  • Use founding-client pricing only as a closing tool, not a default.
  • Send contracts and invoices the same day the call happens.
  • Stack production: every accepted deposit triggers fulfillment.
Days 12–13

Loop

  • Turn every delivery into screenshots, clips, and testimonials.
  • Ask for warm referrals before invoices clear.
  • Anonymize before/after results into a public proof library.
  • Lock in retainer or repeat-buy conversation while trust is hot.

The 3 / 6 / 12 month sprint logic

The same playbook scales across all 10 ideas. First sell proof. Then sell repeatability. Then sell the system.

Months 0–3: Make noise + collect cash

  • Pick one wedge, one buyer, one painfully clear outcome.
  • Send 50–100 targeted outreaches per day between both founders.
  • Use spec work and tiny demos as bait, not free consulting forever.
  • Demand 50–100% upfront on pilots. Your cash gap is the enemy.
  • Turn every delivery into screenshots, clips, testimonials, and referral asks.

Months 4–6: Productize the machine

  • Hire a part-time ops lead before the founders become the bottleneck.
  • Convert one-off packages into retainers, launch calendars, and dashboards.
  • Build a proprietary roster: creators, ambassadors, devs, moderators, editors.
  • Make delivery boring: checklists, templates, QA gates, standard contracts.
  • Raise prices only after delivery is repeatable.

Months 7–12: Own distribution

  • Move from “agency with hustle” to “network with assets.”
  • Package Hawaii as a proof market, then sell mainland expansions.
  • Create a client portal, creator marketplace, template library, or cohort platform.
  • Keep 2–3 flagship case studies extremely polished.
  • Do not scale a low-margin custom service. Scale the repeatable part.
