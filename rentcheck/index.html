RentCheck Hawaii

Turn your renter problem into a paper trail.

A plain-English checklist tool for Hawaii renters dealing with deposits, repairs, notices, neighbor issues, lease confusion, and move-out drama. Not legal advice. Just the next sane step.

Start Here

Five steps. In order. Don't skip.

  1. Choose your renter issue. Pick the closest one in Issue Flows below. If it spans two, pick the most urgent.
  2. Save the right evidence. Photos, videos, dated texts, emails, receipts, lease pages, notices. Original files, not screenshots of screenshots.
  3. Build a timeline. Plain dates and what happened. Use the Evidence Log template. One row per event.
  4. Draft a calm message. Use a Script. Stick to facts and a clear ask. No threats, no name-calling.
  5. Know when to escalate. Legal Aid Society of Hawaii, a mediation center, the court self-help center, or a private attorney — for eviction, lockout, unsafe housing, large deposit disputes, discrimination, violence, or any court papers.

Issue Flows

Pick the closest one. Each card tells you what to save, what to ask, what to watch for, and one calm message to start with.

A

Security Deposit

What to save
  • Signed lease and any addenda
  • Move-in and move-out photos / videos, timestamped
  • Receipts for cleaning, repairs, or pro services
  • Forwarding address you gave the landlord (in writing)
  • Any itemized deposit statement they sent
What to ask
  • When did I move out and when did I give a forwarding address?
  • Did I get an itemized list of deductions in writing?
  • Are deductions for actual damage or for normal wear and tear?
Red flags
  • No itemized statement at all
  • Charges for paint, carpet, "general cleaning" with no detail
  • Vague phone promises and no email reply
First calm message
Hi [Landlord], following up on the security deposit for [address]. I moved out on [date] and provided my forwarding address on [date]. Can you send the itemized statement and the deposit balance? Thanks.
B

Repairs / Unsafe Housing

What to save
  • Photos and videos of the problem, with dates
  • Every repair request you've sent (text, email, portal)
  • Any reply or lack of reply
  • Receipts if you paid to fix anything yourself
  • Medical notes if anyone got sick or hurt
What to ask
  • Is this a habitability issue (no water, no power, mold, leaks, no lock, pests)?
  • Did I report it in writing? When?
  • Has the landlord responded with a date?
Red flags
  • "I'll send someone soon" repeated for weeks
  • Retaliation talk after you complained
  • Being told to fix it yourself with no written agreement
First calm message
Hi [Landlord], writing to document a repair issue at [address]. On [date] I noticed [problem]. Photos attached. Please let me know when this can be fixed. Thanks.
C

Eviction / Notice

What to save
  • Every notice — photograph the paper and the envelope
  • The date and the way it was delivered (taped to door, handed to you, mailed)
  • Any messages about why
  • Receipts and proof of any rent you paid
What to ask
  • What kind of notice is this and what is the deadline on it?
  • Is this a notice to cure (fix something) or a notice to quit (leave)?
  • Have I been served court papers? Is there a hearing date?
Red flags
  • Landlord changing the locks, removing your stuff, or shutting off utilities — that is a lockout, not an eviction
  • Verbal threats only, no paperwork — still document them
  • Court papers you haven't opened yet
First calm message
Hi [Landlord], I received a notice dated [date] regarding [reason]. I'm reviewing it. Can you confirm in writing what you need from me and by when? Thanks.
D

Neighbor / Harassment

What to save
  • Dates, times, and what happened — short factual notes
  • Audio or video only where it's legal and safe to record
  • Texts, voicemails, notes, or DMs from the other party
  • Police report numbers if you called
  • Names and contact info of any witnesses
What to ask
  • Is this a pattern or a single incident?
  • Did I tell the landlord or building manager in writing?
  • Is anyone in physical danger right now?
Red flags
  • Threats of violence, stalking, or weapons — call 911
  • Targeting based on race, gender, disability, family, or country of origin — that's a discrimination issue
  • Landlord refusing to acknowledge written complaints
First calm message
Hi [Landlord/Manager], documenting an issue with the unit at [neighbor address]. On [date / time] [what happened]. I'm asking that this be addressed and that I get a written response. Thanks.
E

Lease Confusion

What to save
  • The full signed lease — every page
  • Any addenda, house rules, HOA rules, pet agreements
  • Texts or emails where the landlord said something different than the lease
  • Receipts for any fees you've paid
What to ask
  • What exactly does the lease say about this?
  • Did the landlord verbally agree to something different?
  • Is there a section number I can quote when I ask?
Red flags
  • Verbal "don't worry about that clause"
  • Fees not listed anywhere in the lease
  • Rules added after move-in with no signed addendum
First calm message
Hi [Landlord], I want to make sure I understand the lease correctly. Section [#] says [quote]. Can you confirm how that applies to [situation]? Thanks.
F

Move-Out Checklist

What to save
  • Written notice you sent and the date you sent it
  • Move-out photos and videos of every room, before keys are handed back
  • Final utility readings and any cleaning receipts
  • Forwarding address you gave in writing
  • Key return method (who, when, how)
What to ask
  • Did I give the required notice in the format the lease asks for?
  • Did I do a walk-through with the landlord?
  • Did I get any walk-through findings in writing?
Red flags
  • Landlord refusing to do a walk-through
  • "We'll figure out the deposit later"
  • No written confirmation that keys were returned
First calm message
Hi [Landlord], confirming move-out from [address] on [date]. Keys returned via [method]. Forwarding address: [address]. Please send the itemized deposit statement when ready. Thanks.

Scripts

Copy, paste, fill in the brackets. Keep it factual. Don't add insults. Don't add threats.

1. Repair request
Hi [Landlord],

I'm writing to document a repair issue at [address, unit #].

On [date], I noticed [describe the problem in one or two plain sentences].
Photos and video are attached.

Please let me know:
- when this can be inspected
- when it will be repaired
- who will be coming and roughly what time

I'd like to keep this in writing so we both have a record. Thanks.

[Your name]
[Phone]
2. Security deposit follow-up
Hi [Landlord],

Following up on the security deposit for [address, unit #].

- Move-out date: [date]
- Keys returned: [date and method]
- Forwarding address provided on: [date]

I haven't received the itemized statement yet. Could you send:
- the itemized list of any deductions
- the remaining deposit balance
- the date it will be sent or deposited

Happy to clarify anything on my end. Thanks.

[Your name]
3. Neighbor / harassment documentation message
Hi [Landlord / Property Manager],

I want to document something happening at [building / unit].

- Date and time: [date, time]
- What happened: [one or two factual sentences, no opinion words]
- Witnesses or recordings: [yes/no, names if any]
- Police involvement: [yes/no, report # if any]

I'm asking that this be addressed and that I get a written response with next steps.
I'll continue to keep notes if anything else happens.

Thanks,
[Your name]
[Unit #]
4. Lease clarification question
Hi [Landlord],

I want to make sure I'm reading the lease correctly before I [action you're considering].

Section [#] / paragraph [#] says:
"[Quote the line from the lease]"

My question:
- Does this mean [your plain-English understanding]?
- If not, can you explain how it applies to [your situation]?

I'd like the answer in writing so I don't act on a misunderstanding. Thanks.

[Your name]

Safety Rules

Read this section before sending anything.

  • RentCheck is not legal advice. It's a checklist tool.
  • Do not ignore court papers or eviction notices. Deadlines are real. Missing them can cost you the case.
  • Do not threaten your landlord. No threats of violence, no threats to damage the unit, no threats to "ruin" anyone online.
  • Do not make claims you cannot document. Stick to what you can prove with photos, video, dated messages, or witnesses.
  • Save written records. Use text and email. If something is said on the phone or in person, follow up in writing: "Just confirming what we discussed today —"
  • Escalate to Legal Aid Society of Hawaii, a mediation center, the court self-help center, or a private attorney for: eviction, lockout, unsafe housing, large deposit disputes, discrimination, violence, or any court papers.
